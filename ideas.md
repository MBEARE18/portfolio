<idea>
  **Design Movement**: Neo-Brutalism / Neubrutalism
  **Core Principles**:
  1.  **Raw & Unapologetic**: High contrast, bold borders, and a lack of traditional "polish" like soft shadows or gradients.
  2.  **Function over Form**: Elements are distinct and separated by hard lines.
  3.  **Playful yet Serious**: Uses a serious layout structure but with playful typography and unexpected color combinations.
  4.  **High Visibility**: Everything is explicitly framed; no floating elements.

  **Color Philosophy**:
  -   **Background**: Stark white (#FFFFFF) or very light grey (#F0F0F0).
  -   **Primary**: Electric Blue (#2563EB) or Vivid Orange (#FF5A1F) for call-to-actions.
  -   **Text**: Pure Black (#000000) for maximum readability and contrast.
  -   **Accents**: Pastel pinks or greens used in "sticker" like elements to break the harshness.
  -   *Reasoning*: To stand out in a sea of clean, minimal portfolios, this style screams confidence and technical prowess without needing to be "pretty."

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  -   **Modular/Bento Grid**: Content is organized into strict, hard-bordered boxes (like a bento box).
  -   **Asymmetry**: The grid isn't perfectly balanced; some boxes are huge, others tiny, creating a dynamic rhythm.
  -   **Sticky Sidebar**: A prominent, hard-bordered sidebar for navigation that stays fixed.

  **Signature Elements**:
  -   **Thick Borders**: 2px-4px solid black borders on everything (cards, buttons, images).
  -   **Hard Shadows**: Buttons and cards have solid, non-blurred drop shadows (box-shadow: 4px 4px 0px #000).
  -   **Monospace Accents**: Use of a coding font (like JetBrains Mono) for tags, dates, and technical details.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  -   **Tactile Feedback**: Buttons depress physically (translate) when clicked, mimicking real switches.
  -   **Hover States**: Elements invert colors or shift position sharply on hover.

  **Animation**:
  -   **Snappy**: No ease-in-out; animations are linear or step-based.
  -   **Marquee**: Scrolling text for skills or announcements.

  **Typography System**:
  -   **Headings**: Archivo Black or Space Grotesk (Heavy, imposing).
  -   **Body**: Public Sans or Inter (Clean, legible).
  -   **Code**: JetBrains Mono (For technical credibility).
</idea>

<idea>
  **Design Movement**: Glassmorphism 2.0 (Aurora UI)
  **Core Principles**:
  1.  **Ethereal & Fluid**: Soft, flowing backgrounds that feel alive.
  2.  **Depth through Translucency**: Layers of frosted glass to create hierarchy.
  3.  **Light & Shadow**: Using inner glows and subtle drop shadows to mimic light passing through glass.
  4.  **Immersive**: The interface feels like a HUD or a floating display.

  **Color Philosophy**:
  -   **Background**: Deep, rich dark mode (Midnight Blue #0F172A) with moving gradients of Cyan, Purple, and Magenta.
  -   **Glass Layers**: White with low opacity (10-20%) and background blur.
  -   **Text**: White and light grey for readability against dark backgrounds.
  -   **Accents**: Neon glows for active states.
  -   *Reasoning*: To present the developer as a "futurist" or "architect of the digital realm." It feels premium and high-tech.

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  -   **Floating Cards**: Content floats in the center, detached from the edges.
  -   **Z-Axis Layering**: Important elements physically sit "higher" (more opaque, brighter borders) than background elements.

  **Signature Elements**:
  -   **Frosted Glass**: `backdrop-filter: blur(12px)` on cards and nav.
  -   **Mesh Gradients**: Backgrounds are not solid but shifting blobs of color.
  -   **Thin Borders**: 1px semi-transparent white borders to define edges of glass.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  -   **Glow Effects**: Hovering over a card makes the border glow or the background shift.
  -   **Parallax**: Mouse movement subtly shifts the background gradients.

  **Animation**:
  -   **Fluid**: Slow, smooth transitions (ease-in-out).
  -   **Float**: Elements gently bob up and down.

  **Typography System**:
  -   **Headings**: Outfit or Plus Jakarta Sans (Geometric, modern).
  -   **Body**: DM Sans (Approachable).
</idea>

<idea>
  **Design Movement**: Swiss Style / International Typographic Style (Digital Adaptation)
  **Core Principles**:
  1.  **Order & Clarity**: Mathematical grids are god.
  2.  **Typography as Image**: Large, bold type is the main visual element.
  3.  **Asymmetry**: Dynamic balance through whitespace and positioning, not centering.
  4.  **Objective Photography**: Use of high-quality, unmanipulated imagery (or abstract geometric shapes).

  **Color Philosophy**:
  -   **Palette**: Strictly limited. Black, White, and one bold accent (e.g., International Orange #FF3300 or Swiss Red).
  -   **Contrast**: Extreme high contrast.
  -   *Reasoning*: To portray the developer as disciplined, organized, and professional. "Code is poetry/structure."

  **Layout Paradigm**:
  -   **Modular Grid**: A visible or invisible multi-column grid (12 or 16 columns).
  -   **Split Screen**: Often uses a 50/50 or 40/60 split between typography and imagery.
  -   **Vertical Rhythm**: Strict adherence to baseline grids.

  **Signature Elements**:
  -   **Huge Type**: Headlines that span the entire width or break words.
  -   **Lines**: Thin horizontal or vertical lines separating sections.
  -   **Geometric Shapes**: Simple circles or squares as decorative elements.

  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  -   **Micro-interactions**: Subtle color shifts on hover.
  -   **Smooth Scroll**: The page flows like a magazine.

  **Animation**:
  -   **Reveal**: Text slides up from behind invisible masks.
  -   **Stagger**: Elements load in one by one along the grid.

  **Typography System**:
  -   **Headings**: Helvetica Now Display or Neue Haas Grotesk (The classic).
  -   **Body**: Helvetica Now Text or Arial (Neutral).
</idea>
